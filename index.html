IRON Wins Capital Markets Deal Architect of the Year at the 2025 BAFI Awards

Lagos, Nigeria — October 2025

Iron Global Markets Limited, a subsidiary of Iron Capital (collectively known as IRON), has been honored with the Capital Markets Deal Architect of the Year (2025) award at the prestigious BusinessDay Banks and Other Financial Institutions (BAFI) Awards.

This recognition reinforces IRON’s reputation as a trailblazer in Nigeria’s financial sector — a firm that combines innovation, precision, and purpose to shape the future of capital markets in Africa.

About IRON

Setting a New Standard

For over a decade, IRON has stood at the forefront of transaction excellence, pioneering innovative deal structures and delivering transformative results for clients across multiple sectors. With each mandate, the firm demonstrates its hallmark approach: deep market insight, flawless execution, and an unrelenting commitment to creating sustainable value.

“At IRON, we don’t just close transactions — we craft solutions that inspire confidence, unlock opportunity, and drive growth,” the company said in a statement. “This award reflects our unwavering pursuit of excellence, innovation, and impact.”

Chairman’s Reflections

Speaking during the event, Mr. Jubril, Chairman of Iron Capital, expressed heartfelt appreciation and reflected on what the recognition means for the firm and its mission.

“This award is more than a trophy; it is a reminder of why we do what we do. At IRON, every deal is a story of possibility — a story of people, ambition, and belief in Africa’s potential. We are driven by the conviction that finance can be a force for transformation, not just transaction. That belief continues to guide everything we build.”

The BAFI Awards, organized annually by BusinessDay Media, celebrate excellence and leadership across Nigeria’s banking and financial services industry. IRON’s recognition in the Capital Markets category underscores its ability to architect complex financial solutions that power businesses, strengthen institutions, and contribute to national development.

As a trusted partner to corporates, investors, and governments, IRON remains focused on redefining how capital works for Africa — providing strategic advisory, innovative deal design, and capital solutions that drive long-term growth.

“We are deeply honored by this recognition and grateful to BusinessDay for the acknowledgment,” the company added. “To our clients and partners — your trust is the foundation on which we build. Together, we are shaping a stronger, more prosperous future for Africa.”

With this distinction, IRON cements its position as one of Africa’s most respected and forward-thinking financial institutions — a true architect of progress in the continent’s capital markets.
